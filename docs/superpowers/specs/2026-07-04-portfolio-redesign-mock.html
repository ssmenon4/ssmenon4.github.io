SHIVASHANKAR MENON / ABOUT

About

I'm Shivashankar, an AI engineer. I've been drawn to the same question since I first saw a Lorenz attractor: how do simple rules produce behavior you couldn't have guessed? These days I ask it of LLM agents instead of differential equations — most recently in Amphoreus, a D&D campaign where every seat at the table is an LLM, built to study how long-horizon plans survive contact with a changing world.

Before agents I worked on other kinds of difficult data — vibration from wheelchairs, ultraviolet foregrounds from a space telescope, retrieval over dense financial text. More recently, I spent a focused two years preparing for India's Civil Services examination: building a holistic understanding of the country's socio-economic issues, public policy, and governance. That research sharpened how I analyse complex, real-world problems — a lens I still reach for as an engineer.

Off the clock I gravitate to media that asks something of you — The Talos Principle is a puzzle game that quietly asks what it means to be human, and that's the kind of thing that stays with me.

I can't tell you where this is all going. But the mark at the top of this page never repeats itself and never leaves its shape, and that seems like a reasonable thing to aim for.
