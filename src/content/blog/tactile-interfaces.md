For two decades we chased flatness. Skeuomorphism gave way to flat design, flat
design gave way to the near-invisible interfaces of today. Somewhere along the way,
we traded warmth for efficiency.

## Why texture matters

Physical metaphors aren't nostalgia — they're cognitive scaffolding. A surface that
looks like it can be pressed invites pressing. A card with a soft, irregular edge
feels handled, considered, human.

> The best interfaces feel less like software and more like well-crafted tools.

## Bringing it back, carefully

The goal isn't to return to glossy buttons and felt textures. It's to reintroduce
*just enough* tactility — grain, organic shapes, gentle motion — to make digital
spaces feel inhabited rather than sterile.
